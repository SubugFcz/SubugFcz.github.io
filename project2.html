PCB for Shunt Current Sensor

Our retired SR3 racecar uses Hall current sensor inside the BMS to measure current in and out. Meanwhile, this type of sensor is too sensitive even to the earth's magnetic field. So it will screw the reading with the car facing north, south, or different angle.

Our future car, SR4, will use Shunt current sensor. My team, under the electrical subteam, was assigned to utilize this sensor in our new vehicle BMS.

In this project, we make a PCB to basically connect the sensor, amplify it using op-amps, and send it to BMS.

Our timeline:

  1. Gather the Shunt sensor and review the datasheet.
  2. Draw the rough schematic for the connection between the sensor and BMS.
  3. Go to the online circuit simulator and calculate the resistor value needed for the broadest reading possible.
  4. Make a better schematic with all the revisions done with KiCad Eeschema.
  5. Simulate the whole system using a breadboard, op-amps, resistors, and linear regulator.
  6. Use a power supply to input Vin and use a multimeter to calculate Vout.
  7. If there's something wrong, we revise the schematic.
  8. Design the PCB with KiCad Pcbnew.
  9. Print the PCB at the Hive!

This is the most complicated yet most exciting project that I've done. From this project, I made many friends, and a huge learning curve for me personally.

We encountered many problems because no one in our teams had any experience with this, but that is what made us learn.
